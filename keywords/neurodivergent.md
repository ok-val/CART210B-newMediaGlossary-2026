## Neurodivergent

Defining neurodivergent is challenging, because it encapsulates so many things. Individuals that fall under that category often have very different experiences, even if they have the same condition. This is because they are spectrums, and every person scales differs. It is an umbrella term that refers to people who neurologically function and process things differently from what is considered the “norm”, which is what is considered “neurotypical”. This term includes conditions such as autism, ADHD, OCD and more. These conditions tend to be characterized by processing sensory information differently, such as having more pronounced senses.[^neurolaunch24neurodivergent]

It is unclear who first coined the term, but its roots do come from the word “neurodiversity”. Judy Singer, an Australian sociologist, is often credited for coining it in 1998. However, we can see some earlier usages. Harvey Blume, a journalist, used it in 1997 and 1998, but rather gave credit of the term to the autistic online community instead, like the “Institute for the Study of the Neurologically Typical”.[^botha24neurodiversity]

Neurodivergent is significant in new media because those who are neurodivergent will approach  digital culture and social media differently, especially because they interpret social niceties in various manners. It is a word that rapidly gained popularity throughout the autistic community. Even today, Its usage still remains relevant, especially in online spaces. There are neurodivergent online communities pretty much on every platform. Having a safe space catered to oppressed and marginalized people allow them to bond and find support.[^ringland19autsome]

Taking autism as an example, its history has changed vastly throughout the years. It was initially “first seen as a variation of schizophrenia and having evolved into autism spectrum disorder (ASD) in the 21st century”. (Lang 2025) Research surrounding neurodivergency is still evolving, and understanding and providing support are improving with the advances are technology.[^lang25history]
